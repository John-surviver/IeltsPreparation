A preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence. It indicates location, time, direction, manner, and other relationships. Prepositions are typically followed by a noun phrase or pronoun called the object of the preposition.


Here are some common prepositions:


1. In: She is sitting in the park.

2. On: The book is on the table.

3. At: I will meet you at the café.

4. By: The painting was created by an artist.

5. With: She went to the movies with her friends.

6. For: He bought a gift for his sister.

7. From: I received a letter from my pen pal.

8. To: We are going to the beach.

9. Over: The bird flew over the house.

10. Under: The cat is sleeping under the bed.

11. Above: The sun is shining above the clouds.

12. Below: The temperature is below freezing.

13. Through: The car drove through the tunnel.

14. Among: They shared the cake among themselves.

15. Between: He sat between his two friends.


Prepositions are crucial for providing spatial, temporal, and relational information in a sentence. They help to establish connections between different elements and indicate how they are related to each other. Prepositions are versatile and can be used in various contexts to express different relationships, such as location, direction, possession, or cause.
