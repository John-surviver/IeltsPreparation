Here's an overview of the band score descriptors for each module:

Listening and Reading:

Band 9: Expert User

  • Shows a full understanding of complex ideas and detailed information.

  • Demonstrates exceptional comprehension skills with accuracy and ease.

Band 8: Very Good User

  • Understands a wide range of demanding texts or conversations.

  • Handles detailed reasoning and inference with few errors.

Band 7: Good User

  • Understands the main ideas and specific details in a variety of contexts.

  • Deals with complex language adequately and independently.

Band 6: Competent User

  • Understands the general meaning in familiar situations.

  • Copes with basic communication in most situations but may make mistakes or misunderstand occasional details.

Band 5: Modest User

  • Understands the main points in familiar topics but may struggle with complex language.

  • Demonstrates some ability to communicate in familiar situations, but errors and misunderstandings are common.

Band 4: Limited User

  • Understands basic information and main points on familiar topics.

  • Uses simple language to communicate but with frequent errors and limited vocabulary.

Band 3: Extremely Limited User

  • Understands only isolated words and phrases.

  • Struggles to comprehend spoken or written language.

Band 2: Intermittent User

  • Has great difficulty understanding and responding to spoken or written language.

Band 1: Non-User

  • No ability to use the language beyond a few words.

Band 0: Did not attempt the test

  • No response or irrelevant response.

Writing:

Band 9: Expert User

  • Shows a high level of proficiency in organizing and presenting ideas.

  • Demonstrates a wide range of vocabulary and accurate use of grammar and punctuation.

Band 8: Very Good User

  • Addresses the task effectively with well-developed ideas.

  • Uses a range of vocabulary and grammatical structures with some errors.

Band 7: Good User

  • Presents a clear position with relevant ideas.

  • Uses a sufficient range of vocabulary and grammar but may have occasional errors.

Band 6: Competent User

  • Addresses the task adequately with some supporting ideas.

  • Demonstrates a basic range of vocabulary and grammar, but errors are noticeable.

Band 5: Modest User

  • Presents a position but may lack coherence and development.

  • Uses basic vocabulary and grammar with frequent errors and limited range.

Band 4: Limited User

  • Attempts to address the task but lacks coherence and clarity.

  • Uses simple language with frequent errors and limited vocabulary.

Band 3: Extremely Limited User

  • Struggles to present a clear position or support ideas.

  • Demonstrates minimal control of vocabulary and grammar.

Band 2: Intermittent User

  • Has great difficulty in organizing and expressing ideas.

Band 1: Non-User

  • No ability to use the language beyond a few words.

Band 0: Did not attempt the test

  • No response or irrelevant response.



Speaking test:

Band 9: Expert User

  • Shows a full command of the language with accuracy and fluency.

  • Uses a wide range of vocabulary and natural, idiomatic language.

  • Speaks with precision, maintaining coherence and cohesion.

Band 8: Very Good User

  • Demonstrates effective command of the language.

  • Shows a wide range of vocabulary and uses it appropriately.

  • Speaks fluently with occasional inaccuracies.

Band 7: Good User

  • Shows good command of the language despite some inaccuracies and occasional misunderstandings.

  • Uses a sufficient range of vocabulary to communicate effectively.

  • Maintains coherence and cohesion, with some effort.

Band 6: Competent User

  • Generally, uses the language adequately, despite noticeable mistakes and misunderstandings.

  • Shows some ability to use complex language but with occasional breakdowns in communication.

Band 5: Modest User

  • Demonstrates partial command of the language, coping with overall meaning but making frequent errors.

  • Struggles to maintain coherence and cohesion, especially in longer responses.

Band 4: Limited User

  • Conveys basic meaning but with frequent inaccuracies and misunderstandings.

  • Struggles to use complex language and may rely on simple sentences.

Band 3: Extremely Limited User

  • Communicates only with isolated words or memorized phrases.

  • Makes numerous errors and fails to maintain coherence.

Band 2: Intermittent User

  • Has great difficulty understanding and producing spoken language.

Band 1: Non-User

  • No ability to use the language beyond a few words.

Band 0: Did not attempt the test

  • No response or irrelevant response.

It's important to note that the band scores for each of the four criteria (Fluency and Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy, and Pronunciation) are combined to calculate an overall speaking band score. The band score reflects the examiner's assessment of your performance based on the specific marking criteria for each band level.
