A proper noun is a word that is used to refer to a specific person, place, or thing. It serves as the unique name for that particular entity. For instance, Mount Everest is a proper noun because it is the name of a specific mountain. Similarly, London, Mumbai, and New York are proper nouns as they denote specific cities.


In contrast, a common noun is a word that refers to a general class or category of people, places, or things. It is not specific to any individual entity. Examples of common nouns include boy (referring to any male child), tree (referring to any plant of its kind), horse (referring to any four-legged mammal of the equine family), king (referring to any male ruler), and town (referring to any urban area smaller than a city).


A collective noun is a word used to represent a group of people, animals, or things as a single unit or entity. It indicates a collection or gathering of individuals. Examples of collective nouns include crowd (a group of people), army (a group of soldiers), team (a group of players), family (a group of related individuals), assembly (a group of people gathered for a common purpose), and committee (a group of individuals assigned to perform a specific task).


Abstract nouns are words that represent concepts, qualities, or actions rather than tangible objects. They signify ideas, emotions, or states of being. Examples of abstract nouns include goodness (the quality of being good), kindness (the quality of being kind), slavery (the state of being enslaved), poverty (the state of being poor), laughter (the action of laughing), theft (the act of stealing), and childhood (the stage of being a child).


Material nouns are words that denote substances or materials from which objects are made. They refer to the physical matter used in construction or manufacturing. Examples of material nouns include gold (a precious metal), wood (a hard material from trees), stone (a solid mineral substance), iron (a strong metallic element), wool (a soft fiber obtained from sheep), bronze (an alloy of copper and tin), and clay (a fine-grained earthy material).
