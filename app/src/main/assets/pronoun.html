Pronoun: A word used in place of a noun to avoid repetition or to refer to a noun already mentioned.


Personal Pronoun: Refers to specific persons or things. Examples: I, you, he, she, it, we, they.


Possessive Pronoun: Shows ownership or possession. Examples: mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, theirs.


Reflexive Pronoun: Reflects back to the subject of the sentence. Examples: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves.


Demonstrative Pronoun: Points to a specific person or thing. Examples: this, that, these, those.


Interrogative Pronoun: Used to ask questions. Examples: who, whom, whose, which, what.


Relative Pronoun: Connects a clause or phrase to a noun or pronoun. Examples: who, whom, whose, which, that.


Indefinite Pronoun: Refers to nonspecific persons or things. Examples: someone, anyone, nobody, everything, anything, nothing.


Reciprocal Pronoun: Indicates a mutual or reciprocal action. Examples: each other, one another.


Intensive Pronoun: Emphasizes a noun or pronoun in a sentence. Examples: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves.
