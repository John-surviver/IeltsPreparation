An adverb is a word that modifies or describes a verb, adjective, or other adverbs in a sentence. It provides additional information about how, when, where, or to what extent an action or quality is performed.


Here are some common types of adverbs along with examples:


1. Adverbs of manner: Describe how an action is performed.

   Example: She danced gracefully.


2. Adverbs of time: Indicate when an action takes place.

   Example: We will go tomorrow.


3. Adverbs of place: Specify where an action occurs.

   Example: The cat is sitting upstairs.


4. Adverbs of frequency: Show how often an action happens.

   Example: He always arrives late.


5. Adverbs of degree: Express the intensity or extent of an action or quality.

   Example: She sings very loudly.


6. Adverbs of reason: Provide the reason or cause for an action.

   Example: He studied hard to pass the exam.


7. Adverbs of affirmation/negation: Indicate agreement or disagreement.

   Example: Yes, I will help you. No, I don't agree.


8. Interrogative adverbs: Used to ask questions about the manner, place, time, reason, etc.

   Example: Where did you go?


9. Relative adverbs: Introduce relative clauses and relate them to a noun or pronoun.

   Example: The park where we played is nearby.


10. Conjunctive adverbs: Connect ideas or clauses together.

    Example: Therefore, we decided to leave early.


It's important to note that some words can function as both adverbs and other parts of speech depending on their usage in a sentence. Adverbs add depth and specificity to the meaning of a sentence by providing additional details about actions, qualities, or circumstances.
